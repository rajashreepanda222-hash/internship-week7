Hypothesis Testing Results

========================================

1. Independent T-Test

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no significant difference between East and West region sales.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
There is a significant difference between East and West region sales.

Test Performed:
Independent Two-Sample T-Test

Decision Rule:
If p-value < 0.05 → Reject H0
If p-value ≥ 0.05 → Fail to Reject H0

Observation:
The test compares average sales performance between East and West regions.

========================================

2. ANOVA Test

Null Hypothesis (H0):
All regions have the same average sales.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
At least one region has a different average sales.

Test Performed:
One-Way ANOVA

Decision Rule:
If p-value < 0.05 → Reject H0
If p-value ≥ 0.05 → Fail to Reject H0

Observation:
The test evaluates whether regional sales performance differs significantly across East, West, North, and South regions.

========================================

Conclusion

The hypothesis tests help determine whether observed differences in sales are statistically significant or occurred due to random variation.

These findings support data-driven business decisions regarding regional performance and sales strategy.
